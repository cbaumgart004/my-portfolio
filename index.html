About Me

Chris has been working in the industry for people with Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities (I/DD) since 2005. For nearly 20 years now, Chris has been working to develop tools, skills, and programs to promote Independence and Agency for the (I/DD) community. Moving into Assistive Technology in 2011, he quickly became one of the most adept programmers working with Communication Devices, specializing in DynaVoxes. As a proof of concept for the DPL (DynaVox Programming Language) for their Series 5 devices, he created his own pirate-themed hangman game in 2013 as a tool for individuals with various degrees of mobility to learn targeted vocabulary in a cooperative environment. You can see a video of it in action here. In recent years, after moving into a position of Technical and Customer Support for CaraSolva, a company that provides an electronic means for companies to record Medication Administration, Chris has begun learning frameworks that will allow him to develop more tools that are more universally accessible.
